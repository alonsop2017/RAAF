## Framework Overview

This assessment framework provides a structured, objective methodology for evaluating candidates for the Enterprise Customer Success Manager position. The framework is designed to ensure consistent evaluation across all candidates while identifying individuals who possess the strategic acumen, relationship management skills, and technical aptitude required for success in a high-growth SaaS environment.

### 5. Job Stability Assessment (10 points)

Evaluates the candidate's employment history based on tenure length in their last three positions. Job stability is an important indicator of commitment, ability to build long-term relationships, and likelihood of retention. Given the strategic nature of this role and the importance of sustained customer relationships, candidates with demonstrated tenure stability are preferred.

#### 5.1 Tenure Scoring Methodology

Calculate the average tenure across the candidate's last three positions. Use the scoring matrix below to assign points.

| Average Tenure (Last 3 Roles) | Points | Risk Level |
|-------------------------------|--------|------------|
| 4+ years average | 10 | Low Risk |
| 3 to 3.9 years average | 8 | Low-Medium Risk |
| 2 to 2.9 years average | 6 | Medium Risk |
| 1.5 to 1.9 years average | 4 | Medium-High Risk |
| 1 to 1.4 years average | 2 | High Risk |
| Less than 1 year average | 0 | Very High Risk |

#### 5.2 Tenure Calculation Worksheet

| Position | Company | Duration | Months |
|----------|---------|----------|--------|
| Most Recent (1) | | | |
| Previous (2) | | | |
| Previous (3) | | | |
| **TOTAL MONTHS** | | | |
| **AVERAGE (÷3)** | | | |

#### 5.3 Contextual Considerations

When evaluating tenure, consider the following factors that may justify shorter tenures:

**Acceptable Reasons for Shorter Tenure:**
- Company acquisition/merger
- Company closure/layoffs
- Relocation for personal reasons
- Significant career advancement opportunity
- Contract/consulting roles (clearly identified)

**Concerning Patterns:**
- Multiple lateral moves
- Repeated departures before 18 months
- Vague explanations for transitions
- Pattern of leaving during challenging periods

**Evaluator Note:** Document any contextual factors that influenced the stability score in the comments section below.

## Appendix: Interview Questions by Category

The following behavioral and situational questions are provided to assist evaluators in gathering evidence for each assessment category.

### Core Experience Questions

1. Describe your experience managing enterprise accounts with 1,000+ employees. What was your book of business value?
2. Walk me through a complex multi-year renewal you owned from start to finish.
3. How do you structure and deliver Executive Business Reviews?

### Technical & Analytical Questions

1. How do you use customer health scores and usage data to prioritize your accounts?
2. Describe a time when data analysis helped you identify an at-risk account. What did you do?
3. What technical aspects of SaaS products are you most comfortable discussing with IT stakeholders?

### Relationship & Communication Questions

1. How do you build relationships with C-level executives at your accounts?
2. Tell me about a difficult conversation you had with a senior stakeholder. How did you handle it?
3. How do you collaborate with Product and Engineering teams to advocate for customer needs?

### Strategic & Business Acumen Questions

1. How do you develop strategic account plans? Walk me through your process.
2. Describe a successful upsell or cross-sell you initiated. How did you identify the opportunity?
3. Tell me about a time you turned around a churn-risk account.

### Job Stability Discussion Points

1. Walk me through your career progression over the past 5-7 years.
2. What prompted your move from [Company A] to [Company B]?
3. What are you looking for in terms of long-term career growth?

### Cultural Fit Questions

1. Describe a time you went above and beyond for a customer without being asked.
2. How do you thrive in fast-paced, high-growth environments with ambiguity?
3. What energizes you most about customer success work?
